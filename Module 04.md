### 1. What is Civic Technology?

Civic technology refers to digital tools and data applications that support democratic participation, government accountability, public service delivery, and community empowerment.

**Examples from Africa:**
- **Ushahidi (Kenya)** — crisis mapping platform used in elections and disaster response
- **BudgIT (Nigeria)** — visualises government budget data for citizens
- **Code for Africa** — builds data journalism and open data tools across the continent
- **AfricTivistes** — civic tech for digital rights and citizen engagement

Civic tech does not have to be complex. A well-designed data visualisation, a clean analysis of public data, or a simple chatbot answering citizens' questions can all have real impact.

### 2. Project Scoping Framework

Use these four questions to define your project:

**1. What is the problem?**
Be specific. Not "healthcare is bad" but "citizens in rural Cameroon lack information about the nearest health facility and its opening hours."

**2. Who is affected?**
Define your user. Who would actually use this tool? What do they need?

**3. What data exists?**
Identify at least one open dataset you could use. Good sources:
- World Bank Open Data: https://data.worldbank.org
- African Development Bank Statistics: https://dataportal.opendataforafrica.org
- OCHA Humanitarian Data Exchange: https://data.humdata.org
- National statistics offices

**4. What would success look like?**
Define one measurable outcome. A working prototype? A published analysis? A dashboard?

### 3. Project Ideas by Theme

**Health & Access**
- Map health facility coverage gaps by region using World Bank data
- Analyse vaccination coverage disparities by income or rural/urban setting

**Governance & Accountability**
- Visualise public budget allocations vs actual spending
- Track election results data over time and surface patterns

**Education**
- Analyse literacy and school completion rates by gender and region
- Build a quiz tool that teaches data literacy concepts

**Environment & Agriculture**
- Analyse rainfall and crop yield correlations using open climate data
- Map access to clean water across Sub-Saharan Africa

**Misinformation & Information Access**
- Build a simple fact-checking classifier (see Module 03 notebook)
- Analyse social media data patterns around a health topic

### 5. Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Adequate (2) | Needs work (1) |
|-----------|--------------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Problem clarity** | Specific, well-scoped civic problem | Clear problem, slightly broad | Problem identified but vague | Unclear what problem is being solved |
| **Data quality** | Real open data, well documented | Real data, some gaps | Simulated data with justification | No clear data source |
| **Technical execution** | Clean, reproducible, well-commented code | Mostly clean, runs end to end | Code runs with some errors | Code incomplete or broken |
| **Visualisation** | Clear, labelled, tells a story | Mostly clear | Charts present but hard to read | No visualisation |
| **Ethics reflection** | Thoughtful discussion of limits and risks | Some reflection | Brief mention | No ethical consideration |
| **Presentation** | Clear, confident, answers questions | Clear with minor gaps | Understandable but unclear | Difficult to follow |
