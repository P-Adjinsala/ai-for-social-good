## Course Description

This course introduces university students to Artificial Intelligence through the lens of social impact and civic technology. Rather than starting with algorithms, we start with questions: *Who does AI serve? Who does it harm? How can we build systems that work for African communities?*

Students will gain both conceptual literacy and practical skills — working with real data, writing Python code, and completing a hands-on civic tech project by the end of the course.

---

## Schedule

### Module 01 — What is AI? (Weeks 1–2)
**Core question:** *Is the algorithm on my phone "intelligent"?*

| Week | Topic | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1 | History of AI — from rule-based systems to deep learning | Lecture + concept mapping exercise |
| 2 | AI in Africa today — mobile money, health diagnostics, agriculture | Case study discussion + notebook |

**Learning outcomes:**
- Define AI, machine learning, and deep learning in plain language
- Identify at least 5 real AI applications currently deployed in Africa
- Distinguish supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning

---

### Module 02 — Data & Society (Weeks 3–4)
**Core question:** *Who collected this data, and who benefits from it?*

| Week | Topic | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 3 | Data collection, consent, and sovereignty | Ethics debate + dataset audit exercise |
| 4 | Bias in data — when the training set lies | Python notebook — detecting bias in survey data |

**Learning outcomes:**
- Explain what training data is and why its composition matters
- Perform basic data cleaning and exploratory analysis in Python
- Apply a data ethics checklist to a real dataset

---

### Module 03 — AI & Misinformation (Weeks 5–6)
**Core question:** *Can a machine tell true from false?*

| Week | Topic | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 5 | How misinformation spreads — platforms, incentives, Africa context | Reading + structured debate |
| 6 | NLP basics — how machines read text | Notebook — build a simple classifier |

**Learning outcomes:**
- Explain TF-IDF and why word frequency matters for text classification
- Train and evaluate a basic text classifier in Python
- Critically assess the limits of automated misinformation detection

---

### Module 04 — Civic Tech Project (Weeks 7–8)
**Core question:** *What problem in your community could data help solve?*

| Week | Topic | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 7 | Project scoping — from problem to data question | Project workshop |
| 8 | Build, present, reflect | Project presentations + peer review |

**Learning outcomes:**
- Define a civic problem that is addressable with data
- Build and present a data analysis or simple AI prototype
- Articulate ethical constraints and limitations of your solution

## Academic Integrity

Students are encouraged to collaborate and discuss. All submitted code and writing must be your own. Use of AI writing assistants must be disclosed — and is itself a topic we will discuss critically in Module 01.
